Temples
Somnath Temple

Somnath Temple is one of the twelve sacred Jyotirlingas dedicated to Lord Shiva. Located on the western coast of Gujarat, this temple has been destroyed and rebuilt several times in history. Its spiritual significance and stunning sea-facing architecture make it a top pilgrimage site.

Dwarkadhish Temple

Dwarkadhish Temple, also known as Jagat Mandir, is a revered temple of Lord Krishna. Located in Dwarka, it is one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites and boasts intricate carvings and a majestic shikhara. The temple draws devotees from across India for its divine aura and coastal setting.

Location favorite Wishlist
Swaminarayan Akshardham Temple

Swaminarayan Akshardham Temple in Gandhinagar is a marvel of pink sandstone architecture. Dedicated to Lord Swaminarayan, it showcases spiritual teachings, exhibits, and cultural values. Its peaceful gardens and intricate carvings make it a spiritual and educational landmark.

Location favorite Wishlist
Ambaji Temple

Ambaji Temple is a major Shakti Peetha, located in the Aravalli hills of North Gujarat. Dedicated to Goddess Amba, it attracts millions of devotees, especially during the Bhadarvi Poonam fair. The temple does not house any idol but worships a sacred Yantra as the main deity.

Sun Temple, Modhera

The Sun Temple at Modhera, built in the 11th century, is dedicated to the solar deity Surya. This architectural marvel features a stunning stepwell and intricately carved pillars. Though no longer used for worship, it remains a cultural gem and heritage monument of Gujarat.

Location favorite Wishlist
Rukmini Temple

Rukmini Temple, located a short distance from Dwarka, is dedicated to Goddess Rukmini, wife of Lord Krishna. This temple is noted for its detailed carvings and peaceful surroundings. Legend and devotion blend here, making it a spiritual complement to Dwarkadhish Temple.

Location favorite Wishlist
Shatrunjaya Jain Temples

Shatrunjaya Hills in Palitana host over 800 intricately carved Jain temples. These temples are considered sacred by the Jain community and require a steep climb of 3,800 steps. Their serene hilltop location and spiritual aura make it a prominent pilgrimage destination.

Location favorite Wishlist
Bala Hanuman Temple

Bala Hanuman Temple in Jamnagar is renowned for its nonstop chanting of “Sri Ram, Jai Ram” since 1964. This continuous devotion has earned it a place in the Guinness Book of World Records. The temple is a major spiritual center and draws pilgrims with its devotional ambiance.

Location favorite Wishlist
Hutheesing Jain Temple

Hutheesing Jain Temple in Ahmedabad was built in the 19th century and is known for its elegant marble structure. Dedicated to the 15th Jain Tirthankara, Dharmanatha, the temple showcases delicate carvings and a calm spiritual environment. It stands as a symbol of Jain artistry and devotion.

Location favorite Wishlist
Nilkanth Dham Swaminarayan Temple

Nilkanth Dham, located in Poicha, is a grand Swaminarayan temple with stunning architecture and spiritual exhibits. The complex features prayer halls, gardens, and light shows that explain the values of Swaminarayan philosophy. Its scenic riverside setting adds to the divine experience for visitors.

Historical Places
Laxmi Vilas Palace

Laxmi Vilas Palace in Vadodara is one of India’s grandest private residences, built by the Gaekwads in 1890. The palace is four times the size of Buckingham Palace and showcases Indo-Saracenic architecture. Its lush gardens, museum, and Durbar Hall make it a royal and cultural attraction.

Location favorite Wishlist
Champaner-Pavagadh Archaeological Park

Champaner-Pavagadh is a UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring a mix of Hindu and Islamic architecture. Set on a historic hill, it includes forts, temples, mosques, and stepwells from the 8th to 14th centuries. Its cultural richness and scenic views make it a top archaeological destination.

Location favorite Wishlist
Rani ki Vav

Rani ki Vav, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Patan, is a grand stepwell built in the 11th century. Commissioned by Queen Udayamati, it features over 1,500 sculptures of gods and goddesses. The stepwell’s symmetry, depth, and carvings make it an architectural and spiritual wonder.

Location favorite Wishlist
Dholavira

Dholavira is one of the most important Indus Valley Civilization sites, located in the Rann of Kutch. It showcases advanced urban planning, water conservation systems, and large reservoirs. This ancient city offers a glimpse into Harappan life and stands as a symbol of early engineering.

Location favorite Wishlist
Statue of Unity

The Statue of Unity is the world’s tallest statue, honoring Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel. Located near Kevadia, it rises 182 meters and includes viewing galleries, exhibitions, and a museum. Surrounded by scenic hills and the Narmada River, it is a proud symbol of Indian unity.

Location favorite Wishlist
Bhujia Fort

Bhujia Fort, built atop Bhujia Hill, is an important military structure of the Jadeja Rajputs. It served as a defense post against invaders and offers panoramic views of Bhuj city. Today, its ruins tell stories of valor, and the climb rewards visitors with a peaceful retreat.

Location favorite Wishlist
Uparkot Fort

Uparkot Fort in Junagadh is an ancient fortress with over 2,000 years of history. It houses Buddhist caves, stepwells, and old cannons within its mighty walls. Legends of sieges and survival make it one of Gujarat’s most intriguing and enduring landmarks.

Location favorite Wishlist
Sidi Saiyyed Mosque

Sidi Saiyyed Mosque in Ahmedabad is renowned for its intricate stone lattice work known as ‘jaali’. Built in 1573, it is one of the finest examples of Indo-Islamic architecture. Its craftsmanship, especially the Tree of Life window, draws art lovers and history buffs alike.

Location favorite Wishlist
Sarkhej Roza

Sarkhej Roza is a beautiful architectural complex located near Ahmedabad, blending Islamic, Hindu, and Jain elements. It includes tombs, palaces, and mosques around a large tank. This peaceful site was a retreat for Sufi saint Ganj Baksh and reflects spiritual harmony and design.

Location favorite Wishlist
Sabarmati Ashram

Sabarmati Ashram in Ahmedabad was Mahatma Gandhi’s residence during the Indian freedom movement. Located on the banks of the Sabarmati River, it served as a hub for non-violent resistance. The ashram now houses a museum and is a place of peace, simplicity, and national history.

Waterfalls
Gira Falls

Gira Falls near Waghai in Dang is a stunning seasonal waterfall in Gujarat surrounded by lush forests. The falls are most attractive during monsoon, when water plunges dramatically from rocky heights. Its scenic beauty and picnic-friendly surroundings make it a monsoon tourist favorite.

Location favorite Wishlist
Girmal Falls

Girmal Falls, the tallest waterfall in Gujarat, is hidden deep in the forests of Dang. Its height and remote natural setting make it a captivating site for nature lovers. The waterfall is active during monsoons, offering misty views and a refreshing forest escape.

Location favorite Wishlist
Zarwani Falls

Zarwani Falls is located within the Shoolpaneshwar Wildlife Sanctuary near Narmada. It is a serene waterfall known for clean waters, rock formations, and surrounding greenery. Ideal for trekking and nature photography, the falls are a peaceful getaway into the wild.

Location favorite Wishlist
Ninai Waterfalls

Ninai Waterfalls is a beautiful and lesser-known natural spot in the Narmada district of Gujarat. Set amidst dense forests and hills, the waterfall is perfect for eco-tourism and trekking. Its unspoiled beauty and cascading waters offer a relaxing experience for all visitors.

Location favorite Wishlist
Barda Waterfalls

Barda Waterfalls, tucked away near Chankhal in Dang, is surrounded by forest and boulder-lined cliffs. The stream forms a gentle cascade making it a tranquil escape into nature. It's ideal for a day trip or picnic, especially during and after monsoon months in Gujarat.

Location favorite Wishlist
Chimer Waterfalls

Chimer Waterfalls is a hidden gem nestled in the forested area of Dediapada in Narmada. It requires a trek through scenic surroundings, rewarding visitors with its untouched charm. The falls are especially vibrant during monsoon, offering scenic views and a calm escape.

Location favorite Wishlist
Hathni Mata Waterfall

Hathni Mata Waterfall, located near Pavagadh, is known for its scenic beauty and cave temple. Surrounded by lush hills, the waterfall flows beside a rock resembling an elephant. It attracts devotees and nature lovers, especially during the rainy season for its greenery.

Location favorite Wishlist
Zanzari Waterfalls

Zanzari Waterfalls is situated on the Vatrak River near Dehgam and is easily accessible by road. The water flows over a rocky terrain, creating shallow pools ideal for wading. It's a great spot for day outings and photography, especially in the early monsoon months.

Location favorite Wishlist
Shiv Ghat

Shiv Ghat, located near Ahwa in Dang, is a natural cascade surrounded by thick forest and rocky terrain. Its peaceful location makes it ideal for spiritual retreats and short treks. This serene water body offers relaxation for nature lovers away from city crowds and chaos.

Location favorite Wishlist
Jamjir Waterfalls

Jamjir Waterfalls, also known as Zamzar Falls, is located near Gir forest in the Gir Somnath district. Its powerful flow and semi-circular drop create a dramatic view for visitors. The area is peaceful and perfect for a weekend getaway amidst the sounds of flowing water.
