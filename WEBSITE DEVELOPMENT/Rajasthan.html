Temples
Birla Mandir (Jaipur)

Birla Mandir in Jaipur is a striking white marble temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu and Goddess Lakshmi. It stands beautifully at the foot of Moti Dungri Hill and is known for its architectural elegance and peaceful ambiance, attracting tourists and devotees alike.

Brahma Temple (Pushkar)

The Brahma Temple in Pushkar is one of the few temples dedicated to Lord Brahma, the creator. It is located near the sacred Pushkar Lake and holds immense spiritual significance, especially during the annual Pushkar Camel Fair and Kartik Poornima festival.

Karni Mata Temple (Deshnoke)

Karni Mata Temple in Deshnoke is world-famous for its sacred rats, known as 'kabbas'. Devotees believe these rats are reincarnated souls and consider them holy. The temple's unique customs and striking marble structure draw curious visitors year-round.

Location favorite Wishlist
Eklingji Temple (Udaipur)

Eklingji Temple, located near Udaipur, is an ancient complex dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple boasts impressive architecture with intricately carved towers and sanctums, and is highly revered by the Mewar royal family and Shiva devotees alike.

Location favorite Wishlist
Galtaji Temple (Jaipur)

Galtaji Temple, also known as the Monkey Temple, is a sacred pilgrimage site nestled in the Aravalli Hills. This temple features natural water springs and kunds, where pilgrims bathe, and it is also home to large groups of friendly monkeys.

Location favorite Wishlist
Mehandipur Balaji Temple (Karauli)

Mehandipur Balaji Temple, located in Karauli, is devoted to Lord Hanuman and is renowned for its exorcism rituals. People from all over India visit seeking relief from evil spirits and black magic, making it a unique and powerful temple.

Location favorite Wishlist
Salasar Balaji Temple (Churu)

Salasar Balaji Temple, located in Churu, is a significant religious destination dedicated to Lord Hanuman. This temple is particularly known for its unique idol and draws massive crowds during fairs held on Hanuman Jayanti and Chaitra Purnima.

Location favorite Wishlist
Rani Sati Temple (Jhunjhunu)

The Rani Sati Temple in Jhunjhunu is a grand complex with no idol of gods, instead honoring Rani Sati. This temple is one of the oldest and wealthiest in India, known for its spiritual ambiance, architecture, and women-centered devotion.

Location favorite Wishlist
Ranakpur Jain Temple (Ranakpur)

Ranakpur Jain Temple is an architectural wonder located in the Aravalli Hills of Rajasthan. Built entirely of marble, it is famous for its 1,444 intricately carved pillars and is dedicated to Tirthankara Adinatha, attracting tourists and pilgrims alike.

Location favorite Wishlist
Jagdish Temple (Udaipur)

Jagdish Temple, located in the heart of Udaipur, is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and stands as a fine example of Indo-Aryan architecture. The temple’s beautiful carvings, tall shikhara, and stone elephants welcome visitors from around the globe.

Historical Places
Jaisalmer Fort

Jaisalmer Fort, also called Sonar Quila or Golden Fort, rises majestically from the Thar Desert in Rajasthan. Built in 1156 AD, it is one of the largest living forts in the world with people still residing inside. Its golden sandstone walls glow under sunlight, making it a visual wonder and cultural treasure.

Location favorite Wishlist
Chittorgarh Fort

Chittorgarh Fort is the largest fort complex in India and a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Rajasthan. It stands as a symbol of Rajput bravery, sacrifice, and honor, with palaces, temples, and towers that echo centuries of heroic tales and sieges.

Location favorite Wishlist
City Palace

City Palace in Udaipur is a grand heritage complex overlooking Lake Pichola, known for its stunning architecture. Built over centuries by the Mewar rulers, it houses museums, courtyards, and royal residences, offering a majestic glimpse into Rajasthan’s regal past.

Location favorite Wishlist
Mehrangarh Fort

Mehrangarh Fort dominates the skyline of Jodhpur from a 400-feet high hill and is among the best-preserved forts in India. Built in the 15th century, it features impressive palaces, courtyards, and museums and offers panoramic views of the Blue City below.

Location favorite Wishlist
Hawa Mahal

Hawa Mahal, or Palace of Winds, is an iconic five-story structure made of red and pink sandstone in Jaipur. Built in 1799, it has 953 small windows allowing royal women to observe street life without being seen. It’s a stunning blend of beauty and purpose.

Location favorite Wishlist
Jal Mahal

Jal Mahal, meaning "Water Palace," is a striking architectural wonder in the middle of Man Sagar Lake in Jaipur. Although entry is restricted, its serene views and reflection in water during sunrise and sunset make it a favorite for photography and sightseeing.

Location favorite Wishlist
Amer Fort

Amer Fort, located in Amer near Jaipur, is a majestic fort known for its artistic blend of Hindu and Mughal architecture. Built with red sandstone and marble, it features courtyards, palaces, and the famed Sheesh Mahal with mirrorwork that glows with minimal light.

Location favorite Wishlist
Nahargarh Fort

Nahargarh Fort stands atop the Aravalli Hills in Jaipur, offering sweeping views of the Pink City. Originally built as a retreat, it later became a stronghold. The fort’s scenic setting and film appearances make it a favorite weekend spot for visitors.

Location favorite Wishlist
Jaigarh Fort

Jaigarh Fort, located on the Cheel ka Teela hill in Jaipur, is famous for housing the world’s largest cannon on wheels—Jaivana. Built for defense purposes, it has underground tunnels and a watchtower with an unbeatable view of the surrounding landscape.

Location favorite Wishlist
Jantar Mantar

Jantar Mantar in Jaipur is a UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring a collection of nineteen astronomical instruments. Built in the 18th century by Maharaja Jai Singh II, it showcases scientific innovation with large sundials and tools to observe celestial bodies accurately.

Waterfalls
Bhimlat Falls

Bhimlat Falls is a hidden gem located in Bundi, Rajasthan. It cascades from about 60 meters and forms a picturesque pool below. Surrounded by rocky cliffs and greenery, it’s a peaceful escape after the monsoon.

Location favorite Wishlist
Menal Falls

Menal Falls is a scenic spot located near Chittorgarh in Rajasthan. Set beside ancient temple ruins, the falls drop powerfully after rains. Its historical charm and natural flow make it a peaceful getaway.

Location favorite Wishlist
Gaipernath Falls

Gaipernath Falls is nestled near Kota and is known for its calm atmosphere. A trek through rocky paths leads to the site, especially lively in monsoon. The waterfall is ideal for photography, picnics, and peaceful views.

Location favorite Wishlist
Chuliya Falls

Chuliya Falls is located in Rawatbhata near Chambal River in Rajasthan. The water flows through deep gorges, forming striking rocky formations. It’s a peaceful site for weekend trips and monsoon sightseeing.

Location favorite Wishlist
Dhrudhiya Waterfalls

Dhrudhiya Waterfalls is a lesser-known beauty in Udaipur’s forest region. It comes alive during the rainy season and offers calm surroundings. The waterfall is great for short treks, family visits, and nature walks.

Location favorite Wishlist
Padajhar Mahadev Waterfalls

Located in Baran, Padajhar Mahadev Waterfalls flows near a Shiva temple. It’s surrounded by greenery and steep rocks, creating a tranquil site. Devotees and travelers visit during monsoon to enjoy both peace and beauty.

Location favorite Wishlist
Damoh Waterfalls

Damoh Waterfalls lies near Sarmathura in Dholpur district of Rajasthan. Falling from great height into a rocky basin, it is stunning post-monsoon. Nature lovers enjoy the raw beauty and rocky cliffs around the site.

Location favorite Wishlist
Garwaji Waterfall

Garwaji Waterfall is located near the Ranthambore region in Rajasthan. It’s a seasonal fall that flows heavily in monsoon and draws local tourists. The peaceful site also has religious significance due to nearby temples.

Location favorite Wishlist
Hathni Kund Waterfall

Hathni Kund Waterfall is found near Mount Abu, Rajasthan’s only hill station. It is surrounded by thick forests and smooth boulders, ideal for trekking. The cool climate and light mist make it a peaceful weekend destination.

Location favorite Wishlist
Jagatpura Waterfall (Kho Nagoriyan)

Jagatpura Waterfall, also called Kho Nagoriyan, is a hidden spot in Jaipur. Active mainly during monsoon, it attracts locals for weekend getaways. Its untouched surroundings and easy trail make it a refreshing retreat.
