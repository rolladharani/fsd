About Kerala:

Kerala, known as "God's Own Country," is a tropical paradise in South India.
It is famous for its backwaters, hill stations, beaches, and rich cultural heritage.
Visitors can enjoy houseboat cruises, Ayurvedic treatments, and explore lush tea plantations.

Best Places to Visit in Kerala

  • Alleppey - Famous for backwater cruises and houseboats.
  • Munnar - A hill station known for tea plantations and scenic landscapes.
  • Kumarakom - A serene backwater destination with bird sanctuaries.
  • Kovalam - A beach paradise with golden sands and lighthouse views.
  • Thekkady - Home to Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary and spice plantations.
  • Wayanad - A nature loverâ€™s retreat with waterfalls and caves.

Local Kerala Cuisines:

  • Appam & Stew - Soft rice pancakes with coconut-based curry.
  • Puttu & Kadala Curry - Steamed rice cakes with black chickpea curry.
  • Kerala Sadya - A traditional feast served on banana leaves.
  • Malabar Biryani - Fragrant rice dish with spices and meat.
  • Karimeen Pollichathu - Pearl spot fish grilled in banana leaves.

Best Seasons to Visit Kerala:

  • Winter (October - February) - Ideal for sightseeing and backwaters.
  • Monsoon (June - September) - Best for Ayurvedic treatments.
  • Summer (March - May) - Great for hill stations like Munnar.
